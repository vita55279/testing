What is Mark Six?

Mark Six is a 6-out-of-49 lottery game organized by the Hong Kong Jockey Club (HKJC). Since its launch in 1975, it has become a cultural staple, with proceeds supporting local charities and government revenue.


  • Cost: HK$10 per single entry
  • Schedule: Draws typically occur every Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday
  • Pool: A single drum containing 49 balls (numbered 1 to 49)

The Drawing Process

In every draw, the HKJC uses a transparent machine to randomly select seven numbers in total.

1. Drawn Numbers: The first 6 balls out of the machine.
2. Extra Number: The 7th ball drawn. This acts as a "bonus" or "half-number" used specifically to determine winners in the 2nd, 4th, and 6th prize tiers.


The Prize Structure

To win, you must match a specific combination of the numbers you chose against the numbers drawn. Unlike some international lotteries, the order in which the numbers are drawn does not matter—only that you have them. Occasionally, the HKJC holds a Snowball Draw (often during festivals like Chinese New Year). They add a pre-existing "Snowball Pool" to the 1st Prize, often pushing the jackpot to over HK$100 Million.
